## Mature women's health

1. NCCIH says there is limited research on detoxes and cleanses and that some may be unsafe. Risks can include dehydration, electrolyte problems, and nutritional issues.
2. The body already has built-in systems that process waste products normally, including the liver, kidneys, gastrointestinal tract, lungs, and skin. That is the evidence-safe correction to vague cleanse marketing.
3. The U.S. Office on Women's Health says menopause treatment can include lifestyle steps, hormonal treatment for some people, and nonhormonal options. Decisions depend on symptoms, timing, and risk factors.
4. The Office on Women's Health also says hormone therapy should be used at the lowest dose for the shortest time needed when it is chosen, and some women should avoid it depending on stroke, clot, liver, cancer, or cardiovascular risk history.
