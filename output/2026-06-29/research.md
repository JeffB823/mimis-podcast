## Kyoto

1. Kyoto City's new-items page for June 29, 2026 shows a交通局 notice titled "定期券の発売体制の見直し." The notice says Kyoto will reorganize commuter-pass sales from August, moving IC pass issuing machines to the busier Kitaoji Station and Kyoto Station counters and ending sales at the Nishigamo and Yokooji offices after August 1. For listeners, this is a practical reminder that routine transit errands will change even before autumn.
2. Kyoto City Transportation Bureau also published on June 29, 2026 that FY2025 preliminary ridership rose above the previous year for both city buses and the subway, and combined daily ridership edged above the pre-pandemic baseline for the first time. The practical consequence is simple: buses and stations are feeling busy again, so summer movement around the city will likely keep that crowded baseline.
3. Kyoto City announced on June 26, 2026 that "京の七夕2026" will collect wishes at related venues across Kyoto Prefecture from July 1 through August 31. It is a lighter culture item that gives the show a grounded seasonal handoff without overselling tourism.

## Mature women's health

1. NCCIH says research on detoxes and cleanses is limited and some approaches may be unsafe.
2. Evidence-based guidance does not support vague marketing claims that the body needs extreme cleansing; the liver, kidneys, lungs, skin, and gastrointestinal tract already handle normal waste processing.
3. NCCIH notes possible risks such as dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, and inadequate nutrition depending on the method used.
4. The Office on Women's Health says menopause treatment options can include lifestyle measures, menopausal hormone therapy, and nonhormonal medicines, depending on symptoms and risk factors.
5. The same federal guidance says hormone therapy should be used at the lowest dose that works for the shortest time needed when it is chosen.
6. A safe action for today's segment is symptom tracking rather than self-prescribed cleansing: jot down sleep, hot flashes, sweating, mood, palpitations, and any pattern that would help a clinician assess what is actually changing.
