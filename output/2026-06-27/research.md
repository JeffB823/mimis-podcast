## U.S. national news

1. The U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis reported on June 26, 2026 that the Personal Consumption Expenditures price index rose 0.1% in May and 2.3% from a year earlier. Core PCE, which excludes food and energy, also rose 0.2% on the month and 2.7% year over year. For listeners, the practical consequence is that inflation has not disappeared, especially in core services, so interest-rate expectations and household costs remain sensitive to the next few reports.
2. U.S. Central Command said on June 26, 2026 that American forces struck Iranian-backed Houthi targets in Yemen in what it described as self-defense after attempted attacks on a U.S.-flagged cargo vessel in the Red Sea. This matters because shipping security and U.S. military posture in the region remain active even when domestic attention shifts elsewhere.
3. The White House said on June 26, 2026 that President Donald Trump will travel to Iowa on July 3 to kick off a yearlong celebration of the 250th anniversary of the United States. This is lighter than the economic and security stories, but it is a visible signal of how the administration plans to frame the run-up to next summer's semiquincentennial observances.

## Virginia Beach

1. The City of Virginia Beach published its July 4 operating schedule on June 26, 2026. City offices will be closed Friday, July 3, and refuse and recycling collection will move one day later than usual. For listeners, this is a practical calendar item: trash pickup, office services, and holiday timing will all shift next week.
2. The city also promoted Fourth of July oceanfront programming, including concerts and fireworks, in a June 26, 2026 announcement. The listener-facing value is simple: busier traffic, heavier beach crowds, and a good reason to plan parking and arrival time early if going to the resort area.

This falls just outside the freshest city-hall cycle but is still a meaningful local development because it speaks to visible housing pressure and what the city and partners are building in response.

2. Kyoto City and Kyoto Bus announced on June 26, 2026 that the "京の七夕 2026" bus tours will run from August 1 through August 31, including evening routes tied to the summer light-up season. This is a local-life and culture item with near-term planning value for residents and visitors.

## Mature women's health

1. NCCIH says there is not much research on detox diets and cleanses and that some programs can be unsafe, especially when they involve fasting, laxatives, or restrictive liquid-only plans. Key risks include dehydration, electrolyte imbalance, inadequate nutrition, and interactions with medicines.
2. The body already has systems that process wastes and byproducts normally, including the liver, kidneys, gastrointestinal tract, lungs, and skin. This is the evidence-safe corrective to marketing claims that a cleanse is needed to remove unnamed toxins.
3. The U.S. Office on Women's Health says menopause symptoms can be managed in different ways, including lifestyle steps, hormone therapy for some people, and nonhormonal options depending on symptoms and medical history. A Menopause Society press release from June 18, 2026 described an observational study linking hormone-therapy use with healthier reported lifestyle patterns, but it did not prove causation.

Featured title: Bonjinsha's upcoming 『読むための図解日本語［表現・文章編］ 読解に大切な12の文法』. The publisher's listing describes an early July 2026 release aimed at learners around A2 to JLPT N3 and above.
