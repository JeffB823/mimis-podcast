# Mimi's Podcast — Research dossier

Editorial date: June 25, 2026 (America/New_York)

## U.S. national news

1. The U.S. Supreme Court issued two 6–3 immigration decisions favorable to the Trump administration. One permits termination of Temporary Protected Status for people from Haiti and Syria, potentially affecting more than 350,000 people. The other clears the way for reviving “metering,” which limits how many asylum seekers are processed at ports of entry. Both stories were published by AP on June 25.
2. The Supreme Court also struck down Hawaii’s default ban on carrying guns into privately owned public-facing places such as stores and hotels unless the owner had expressly permitted it. The 6–3 ruling leaves property owners able to prohibit guns.
3. Senate Republicans rejected a war-powers resolution related to the Iran conflict by 47–50–1 after a tense meeting with President Donald Trump. Separately, the president delayed signing a broadly bipartisan housing-supply bill while seeking proof-of-citizenship voting legislation.

## Virginia Beach

1. Virginia’s expanded ban on restaurant polystyrene foam cups and takeout containers begins July 1. WTKR reported on June 25 that smaller Virginia Beach restaurant owners are concerned about replacement costs.
2. Virginia Beach’s FY2027 budget and new fee schedule take effect July 1. The $2.886 billion city-and-schools budget includes up to $80 in personal-property-tax relief per vehicle and an approximately 3.5% city employee raise.
3. Virginia Beach police said no charges were filed after the June 22 vehicle barricade at the Strawbridge shopping center. Police deployed tear gas, took the driver into custody, and reported no injuries.

## Kyoto

1. A magnitude 3.1 earthquake occurred in southern Kyoto Prefecture at about 10:44 p.m. Japan time on June 25. Maximum intensity was 2 in Kameoka; several Kyoto wards recorded intensity 1. No tsunami risk was reported.
2. Kyoto City announced stronger litter countermeasures for the Gion and Kiyamachi areas during the Gion Festival period.
3. Kyoto City announced crowdfunding and early exhibitor information for the 15th Kyoto International Manga Anime Fair, and an event allowing participants to experience operating a new Karasuma Subway Line Series 20 train.

## Professional sumo

1. The June 13–14 Paris exhibition concluded with ozeki Kotozakura defeating yokozuna Hoshoryu by yorikiri in the overall championship bout.
2. The next honbasho is the Nagoya tournament at IG Arena, July 12–26.
3. The new banzuke is scheduled for June 29.

## Los Angeles Dodgers

1. Los Angeles beat Minnesota 4–3 on June 24 and completed a three-game sweep.
2. Mookie Betts hit his 300th career home run and finished 3-for-4, a triple short of the cycle.
3. Shohei Ohtani pitched six innings with eight strikeouts. The Dodgers were 52–29, first in the NL West and nine games ahead of San Diego. Their next game is June 26 at San Diego, with Roki Sasaki scheduled against Walker Buehler.

## Mature women’s health

1. A Menopause Society release dated June 18 described a large observational study in which menopausal women taking hormone therapy also reported healthier diet, physical activity, and sleep patterns. This is an association and does not show that hormone therapy caused the healthier lifestyle.
2. NCCIH says convincing research supporting detox diets for weight management or toxin removal is limited, and some detox or cleanse programs may be unsafe or falsely advertised.
3. Menopause treatment should be individualized. Government and professional guidance recognizes lifestyle measures, hormone therapy, and nonhormonal medicines as possible tools depending on symptoms and personal risk.
